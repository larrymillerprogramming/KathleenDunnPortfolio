A New Jersey native, Kathleen earned her Bachelor of Music in Music Education from Westminster Choir College in Princeton, New Jersey. During her time at Westminster, she performed frequently with the Westminster Symphonic Choir, directed by Dr. Joe Miller, and the Grammy® nominated Westminster Williamson Voices, led by Dr. James Jordan. As a member of Williamson Voices, Kathleen recorded three albums (Hole in the Sky, Carolae: Music for Christmas, and Silence Into Light) and participated in the annual Choral Institute at Oxford, directed by Dr. James Jordan and Dr. James Whitbourn. During the 2015 Institute, Kathleen performed as the mezzo-soprano soloist for Leonard Bernstein’s Chichester Psalms in a performance commemorating the 50th Anniversary of the work's premiere. Other performance highlights include the world premiere of Paul Mealor’s Symphony No. 1: Passiontide in Scotland, Gustav Mahler’s Symphony No. 8 with the Philadelphia Orchestra and Yannick Nézet-Séguin at the Kimmel Center, and Arvo Pärt's Kanon Pokajanen at the Metropolitan Museum of Art.

Following her undergraduate studies, Kathleen earned a Postgraduate Diploma in Music Education and Choral Conducting from the Kodály Institute of the Liszt Academy of Music in Kecskemét, Hungary. During her year at the Institute, she was able to focus on musicianship and solfege training, had the opportunity to observe the Kodály method in Hungarian classrooms, and benefitted personally and artistically from studying alongside musicians from 18 different countries. She sang in ensembles with Dr. Árpád Tóth and Péter Erdei.

Kathleen is a member of the American Choral Directors Association, the National Association for Music Education, the Organization for American Kodály Educators, and Sigma Alpha Iota. Her primary conducting mentors have been Dr. James Jordan and Péter Erdei.
